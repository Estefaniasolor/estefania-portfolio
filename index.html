Estefania Solorzano

Dance Artist | Choreographer | Anthropologist

About

Estefania Solorzano is a multidisciplinary dance artist and cultural anthropologist whose work bridges traditional and contemporary forms of movement. With a career spanning performances, choreography, and anthropological research, Estefania explores themes of memory, identity, and heritage.

She has performed internationally, collaborating with renowned companies like Border Crossings (UK) and La Fábrica (Ecuador), and has presented her own choreographic works, including El Abrazo (2020) and Dance Diaries (2022). Her research projects, such as Forgetting Flowers, integrate movement and anthropology to investigate the embodiment of memory and forgetfulness.

Anthropological Research Projects

Forgetting Flowers (2019–Present)

A multidisciplinary project integrating anthropological and movement research to investigate memory, embodiment, and the relationship between bodily experiences and forgetfulness.

Dans Humains

Co-founded a research group exploring cross-disciplinary projects in dance and anthropology, emphasizing the intersection of embodied practices and cultural analysis.

Zagreb International Folklore Festival (2019)

Invited as an international guest to teach the Ecuadorian traditional dance "Andarele," promoting cultural exchange and understanding (Croatia).

Greek Dance Influence Research (2018)

Conducted an in-depth analysis of traditional Greek dances, exploring their influence on the development of the contemporary dance technique Apogeos (Greece).
